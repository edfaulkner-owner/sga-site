About SGA

Archive • Preserve • Record

Our Mission

Sticker Grading Authority is an independent archive dedicated to documenting, preserving, and organizing sticker culture. SGA records physical specimens, population data, field observations, and vending machines so that the history surrounding them is not lost.

The SGA Archive

The SGA Archive brings together graded stickers, documented collections, Urban Relics, Forgotten Relics, Wild Specimens, and other records connected to sticker culture. Not every archived item receives a grade. Some are preserved through photography, location data, observation notes, and permanent registry records.

Authentication & Registry

Stickers submitted for grading are evaluated through a structured process examining surface condition, print and color integrity, centering and cut accuracy, adhesive condition, backing condition, and overall presentation. Authenticated specimens may be encapsulated and entered into the SGA Registry with a unique identification number.

The Field Log

The SGA Field Log allows contributors to document stickers encountered outside traditional collections. Observations may be classified as Urban Relics, Forgotten Relics, or Wild Specimens and recorded with photographs, location information, surface details, and field notes.

The Machine Registry

The Machine Registry documents sticker vending machines through submitted observations, photographs, locations, machine details, and status updates. Each documented machine becomes part of an expanding visual network shown on the SGA map.

Why Spiders?

Spiders build patient, interconnected webs that reveal relationships others may overlook. The SGA spider represents observation, documentation, and the growing network connecting stickers, machines, locations, collectors, and archival records.

Recorded • Not Valued

SGA documents what exists and preserves the information surrounding it. Registry records and population data are not financial appraisals, price guides, or declarations of market value. The purpose of SGA is to serve the historical record.
